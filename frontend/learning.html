Understanding Debt and Fixed Income

This section is for educational purposes only. Understanding the concepts below is crucial for navigating the debt market.

What is a Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD)?

An NCD is a financial instrument that certifies a company has borrowed money from the issuer. It is 'non-convertible,' meaning it cannot be converted into equity shares. They are a common form of corporate debt in India, offering fixed income (coupon payments) over a defined tenure.

The Role of Credit Ratings (AAA, AA, etc.)

Credit ratings are assigned by SEBI-registered rating agencies (like CRISIL, ICRA, CARE). They assess the financial health of the issuer and their ability to repay the debt. They indicate the relative safety of the bond:

  • **AAA:** Highest safety, very low credit risk.
  • **AA:** High safety, low credit risk.
  • **A:** Adequate safety, moderate credit risk.

*Note: A credit rating is an opinion and not a recommendation to buy or sell a security.

The Difference Between Coupon and Yield

The **Coupon Rate** is the fixed interest rate the issuer pays the bondholder based on the face value. The **Yield to Maturity (YTM)** is the total return anticipated on a bond if it is held until maturity. YTM changes daily with the bond's market price, while the coupon rate is fixed. For research purposes, the YTM is the forward-looking metric often used by investors, but we focus on the factual coupon rate.
